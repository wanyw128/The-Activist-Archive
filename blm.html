Black Lives Matter

  • Summary
  • Timeline
  • Resources

Source: wayne.edu

The Black Lives Matter (BLM) movement began in 2012 as a hashtag after the shooting death of a Black-American man named Trayvon Martin. This organization aims to provide an equitable and safe space to improve Black lives by eradicating police violence and systemic racism. Despite highlighted cases of brutality occuring in the United States, BLM has gained allies worldwide following the deaths of Michael Brown in Missouri and Eric Garner in New York in the year 2014 and the George Floyd tragedy of 2020. Media coverage of BLM protesting strategies has also helped the organization gain supporters and instigate policy changes as the movement continues to grow and fight for racial equity.

On July 13, 2021, the BLM organization reminds supporters of the social media posts that began this social justice movement.
