    • Anti Asian American and Pacific Islander Hate

      Anti AAPI Hate is an organization whose goal is to build a multiracial movement to fight against systemic racism and establish equity. This website provides mental health and educational resources for educators and individuals. It also allows viewers to report incidents against AAPI, and shares data collected about these reports.

      AAPI Equity Alliance

      AAPI Equity Alliance improves the lives of AAPI by advocating for policy changes and civic engagement. Read about their initiatives, including their COVID-19 Health Grants, explore the website's newsletters, and use the COVID-19 resources available on their page.

      OCA-Asian Pacific American Advocates

      OCA-Asian Pacific advocates for AAPI civil rights. Their mission is to advance the social, political, and economic well-being of Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders in the United States. Partipate in OCA-Asian Pacific American Advocates programs, which are inclusive to various age groups, find and join your local chapter, or read released articles in their "Newsroom".

      AAARI

      The Asian American / Asian Research Institute is a university-wide scholarly research and resource center established by the CUNY Board of Trustees that focuses on policies and issues that affect Asians and Asian Americans. It covers four areas: Asian American Studies; East Asian Studies; South Asian Studies; and Trade & Technology Studies. Access the many programs, scholarships and awards, events, and resources provided by the AAARI. Learn from their lectures and read their publications. The AAARI also shares a timeline highlighting the achievements, policies, and other notable historical moments concerning Asian Americans and Asians.

      Asian American Studies Online

      Asian American Studies Online is the first electronic online commons for scholars, practitioners, artists, and activists who are committed to doing and disseminating research and transcultural perspectives on Asian and Pacific American Studies. View news articles, dialogues and interviews, and perspectives from theorists, activists, and communities involved in Asian American and Pacific American studies.

      Chinese-American Planning Council

      The Chinese-American Planning Council CPC is a social service organization whose mission is to serve and empower Asian American, immigrant, and low-income communities in New York City by ensuring they have equitable access to the resources and opportunities needed to thrive.

      AsAmNews

      AsAmNews is a community of users interested in reading, learning and commenting on news, events, people & issues in the Asian Americans and Pacific Islander communities. The site is dedicated to documenting the Asian American experience to showcase its depth and diversity. Here you will find a full roundup of headlines and blogs with original reporting along with aggregated headlines from both mainstream and ethnic media.

      Asian Pacific American Labor Alliance (APALA)

      APALA is dedicated to promoting political education and voter registration programs among AAPIs, and to the training, empowerment, and leadership of AAPIs within the labor movement and APA community. APALA works to defend and advocate for the civil and human rights of AAPIs, immigrants and all people of color, and continues to develop ties within international labor organizations, especially in the Asia-Pacific Rim.
      Through this website, you can connect to a regional APALA chapter, support APALA by joining their membership, and access a multitude of COVID-19, protest, and anti Asian violence toolkits and resources. Sign up for their newsletter, job opportunities at APALA, and external opportunities in the labor movement. Donate to or inquire for a COVID-19 community fund, read the stories of AAPI workers, or join one of APALA engaging and uplifting events.

      Asian American Organizing Project

      The mission of the Asian American Organizing Project is to empower young Asian Minnesotans to create systems change for an equitable, conscious, and just society. Through this initiative, young Asian Minnesotans (14 – 35 years old) will lead long-term racial, economic, and gender justice through reflective leadership, co-creating community change, and amplifying narratives. Through this resource, users can access resources, volunteer at Asian American Organizing Project’s many programs, and read AAPI stories and blogs.

      18 Million Rising

      18 Million Rising has the vision of educating, inspiring, and mobilizing young Asian Americans to fight against the underrepresentation of Asian Americans in the United States. Learn about featured AAPI campaigns or pitch a campaign yourself through this resource.
